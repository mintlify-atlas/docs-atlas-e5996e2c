## Overview

The Mango Damage Detection model classifies mango images into **5 distinct categories** based on visual characteristics. The model is trained to identify common diseases and conditions affecting mango fruit quality.

```python
# From fastapi_app/api.py
class_names = [
    "Anthracnose", 
    "Bacterial-Black-spot", 
    "Fruitly", 
    "Healthy-mango", 
    "Others"
]
```

## Detailed Damage Descriptions

### 1. Anthracnose

<Accordion title="About Anthracnose">
**Causal Agent:** *Colletotrichum gloeosporioides* fungus

**Characteristics:**
- Dark brown to black circular or irregular lesions
- Sunken spots that may expand as the fruit ripens
- Pink spore masses may appear in humid conditions
- Can affect both pre-harvest and post-harvest mangoes

**Impact:**
- Major post-harvest disease causing significant economic losses
- Reduces shelf life and market value
- Can spread rapidly in storage facilities

**Visual Indicators:**
- Small dark spots that enlarge and coalesce
- Lesions become sunken as fruit tissue collapses
- May have a slightly raised border
</Accordion>

### 2. Bacterial Black Spot

<Accordion title="About Bacterial Black Spot">
**Causal Agent:** *Xanthomonas* bacteria species

**Characteristics:**
- Small black spots with water-soaked margins
- Yellow halo may surround the lesions
- Spots are usually raised initially, then become flat or sunken
- More prevalent during rainy seasons

**Impact:**
- Affects both fruit appearance and quality
- Can lead to secondary infections
- Reduces marketability and consumer acceptance

**Visual Indicators:**
- Multiple small black spots across the fruit surface
- Characteristic yellow halo around spots
- Spots may crack and provide entry for other pathogens
</Accordion>

### 3. Fruitfly Damage (Fruitly)

<Accordion title="About Fruitfly Damage">
**Causal Agent:** Fruit fly species (*Bactrocera* and *Ceratitis*)

**Characteristics:**
- Small puncture marks from oviposition (egg-laying)
- Soft, discolored areas where larvae feed internally
- Premature fruit drop may occur
- Internal tunneling by larvae

**Impact:**
- Makes fruit unmarketable and inedible
- Quarantine restrictions in many countries
- Significant economic pest in mango production

**Visual Indicators:**
- Tiny pin-prick holes on fruit surface
- Discolored, soft patches
- May show signs of decay around entry points
- In advanced stages, larvae exit holes may be visible
</Accordion>

### 4. Healthy Mango

<Accordion title="About Healthy Mangoes">
**Characteristics:**
- Smooth, unblemished skin
- Uniform coloration appropriate to variety and ripeness stage
- No visible lesions, spots, or physical damage
- Firm texture without soft spots

**Quality Indicators:**
- Clear, glossy surface
- Natural variation in color based on variety (green, yellow, red)
- No signs of disease or pest damage
- Suitable for fresh market or processing

**Market Value:**
- Premium pricing for undamaged fruit
- Longer shelf life and storage potential
- High consumer acceptance
</Accordion>

### 5. Others

<Accordion title="About Other Damage Types">
**Category Purpose:**
This catch-all category includes damage that doesn't fit the four main classes:

**Possible Conditions:**
- Mechanical damage (bruising, cuts, abrasions)
- Sunburn or heat damage
- Nutritional deficiencies
- Other diseases not in the main categories
- Ambiguous or multiple damage types
- Poor image quality preventing clear classification

**Interpretation:**
- Requires further visual inspection
- May indicate rare or emerging disease types
- Could be a combination of multiple damage factors
</Accordion>

## Understanding Predictions

### How the Model Makes Predictions

The model uses a softmax output layer to generate probability scores for each class:

```python
# From fastapi_app/api.py
# Predict using the model
predictions = model.predict(image_array)
# Output shape: (1, 5) - batch of 1, 5 class probabilities

# Get the predicted class index with highest score
predicted_class_index = np.argmax(predictions, axis=1)[0]

# Map to class name
predicted_class_name = class_names[predicted_class_index]
```

### Example Prediction Flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Image Input">
    User uploads a mango image (any size, RGB format)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Preprocessing">
    Image is resized to 224×224 and normalized to [0, 1]
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Model Inference">
    VGG19 model outputs probability distribution:
    ```python
    # Example output
    predictions = [
        [0.05, 0.82, 0.03, 0.08, 0.02]  # Probabilities for each class
    ]
    ```
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Class Selection">
    Highest probability determines the predicted class:
    ```python
    # Index 1 (Bacterial-Black-spot) has highest probability (0.82)
    predicted_class = "Bacterial-Black-spot"
    ```
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Result">
    JSON response returned to Django:
    ```json
    {"prediction": "Bacterial-Black-spot"}
    ```
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Making Direct API Calls

You can test the FastAPI endpoint directly using curl:

```bash
curl -X POST "http://localhost:8001/predict/" \
  -H "accept: application/json" \
  -H "Content-Type: multipart/form-data" \
  -F "file=@/path/to/mango_image.jpg"
```

Or using Python's `requests` library:

```python
import requests

with open('mango_image.jpg', 'rb') as f:
    files = {'file': f}
    response = requests.post(
        'http://localhost:8001/predict/',
        files=files
    )
    
if response.status_code == 200:
    result = response.json()
    print(f"Predicted damage: {result['prediction']}")
```

## Interpreting Results

<Note>
**Important:** The model returns only the class label with the highest probability. It does not provide confidence scores or probability distributions in the current implementation.
</Note>

### Decision Guidelines

Based on the prediction result, here are recommended actions:

| Prediction | Recommendation |
|------------|----------------|
| **Healthy-mango** | Suitable for market sale, storage, or processing |
| **Anthracnose** | Isolate from healthy fruit; use immediately or discard if severe |
| **Bacterial-Black-spot** | Remove from healthy stock; may be suitable for processing if minimal |
| **Fruitly** | Discard immediately; do not store with other fruit |
| **Others** | Requires manual inspection to determine appropriate action |

### Confidence Considerations

<Warning>
While the model is trained on mango damage data, consider these factors:

- **Lighting conditions:** Poor lighting can affect classification accuracy
- **Image quality:** Blurry or low-resolution images may reduce performance
- **Multiple damage types:** Fruit with multiple issues may be classified as "Others"
- **Variety differences:** Some mango varieties may have natural coloring that resembles damage
- **Stage of damage:** Early-stage infections may be harder to detect

For critical decisions (e.g., export quality control), combine model predictions with manual inspection.
</Warning>

## Model Limitations

<Accordion title="Known Limitations">
1. **Single-label classification:** The model assigns only one class, even if multiple damage types are present

2. **No confidence scores:** The API doesn't currently expose probability scores (only the top prediction)

3. **Training data bias:** Model performance depends on the diversity and quality of training images

4. **New disease types:** Emerging diseases not in the training set will likely be classified as "Others"

5. **Image requirements:** Best results require:
   - Clear focus on the mango
   - Adequate lighting
   - Visible damage areas
   - Minimal background clutter
</Accordion>

## Improving Classification Accuracy

<Tip>
**Best Practices for Image Capture:**

- Use natural daylight or consistent artificial lighting
- Capture images at multiple angles if damage is localized
- Ensure the mango fills most of the frame
- Use a minimum resolution of 224×224 pixels (higher is better)
- Avoid shadows that obscure damage areas
- Photograph damaged areas close-up for better feature visibility
</Tip>
